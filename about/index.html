    • General

    I am a doctoral student researcher and National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow in the Social and Affective Neuroscience Lab at the University of Oregon (UO). I am affiliated with the UO Center for Translational (Neuro)Science, an organization focused on translating basic psychological and physiological science into actionable programs of research that have the potential to positively impact the surrounding community.

    Prior to my graduate studies, I was a full-time project coordinator on an NIH-funded study investigating the feasibility of reversing deficits in inhibitory control associated with early life adversity; a collaboration between Drs. Berkman and Fisher in the University of Oregon Department of Psychology.

    Research

    My research interests center around two related questions:

    1. What leads to success or failure in the pursuit of personal goals?
    2. How do people make choices about health?

    Using neuroimaging and behavioral data, I study how targeted and personalized health interventions might help people succeed in acheiving the health goals they set for themselves. I intend to build on this program of research with a focus on preventative health behaviors (e.g. healthy eating, exercise, immunization, cancer screenings).

    Code and Tutorials

    I also create open data analysis code and training materials in R and Python for use by fellow researchers. Tutorials can be found on this site and all associated code is available on GitHub.
